(Picture Courtesy: Facebook) The wait for 'Coolie' finally ended on August 14, but it wasn’t just Rajinikanth’s high-voltage presence that had fans talking. A leaked clip from the Lokesh Kanagaraj directorial revealed Aamir Khan’s jaw-dropping cameo, and the internet hasn’t stopped buzzing since.Spoiler Alert: This article contains significant plot details and spoilers for 'Coolie'. If you haven't seen the film yet and wish to avoid spoilers, please stop reading now.Stepping in as “Dahaa” during the film’s climax, Aamir’s entry — complete with razor-sharp dialogue delivery and comic swagger — had many calling it his best on-screen arrival yet. Though his role lasts under 10 minutes, the dynamic between him and Rajinikanth crackled with energy, giving the audience an unexpected blend of menace and humour. The twist? Dahaa is not just an antagonist; he’s also a fanboy of Rajinikanth’s character, a detail that drew plenty of laughs and applause inside theatres.Netizens reactOne among the first viewers Tweeted, "Aamir Khan in Coolie Trailer is pure mass, one of his massiest avatars ever, and easily among the boldest by any Bollywood actor. This is what happens when you present Aamir right. Hats off to Lokesh for unlocking Beast Mode Aamir." Another one commented, "Aamir Khan."(Picture Courtesy: X)PollDid the fanboy twist in Aamir Khan's character surprise you?Yes, it was a great twist!No, I expected itBox office battle with ‘War 2’ off to a fierce startDespite clashing with the Hrithik Roshan–Jr NTR action spectacle 'War 2', 'Coolie' charged into theatres with unstoppable momentum. Early estimates from Sacnilk peg its day-one earnings at Rs 18.5 crore nett in India — a number expected to climb after evening and night shows wrap up. Advance bookings had already hinted at a roaring start.Fans turn release day into a festivalIn some theatres, the excitement hit such a peak that screenings were momentarily halted during Rajinikanth’s entry scene, just so fans could whistle, cheer, and capture the moment on their phones. Outside, towering cutouts of the superstar were adorned with garlands, while inside, the roar of the crowd rivaled the soundtrack.With its mix of mass action, a fan-service cameo, and Thalaivar's
