(Getty Images) NEW DELHI: Team India wicketkeeper-batter Rishabh Pant has been ruled out of action since the fourth Test of India's recently concluded Anderson-Tendulkar Trophy series against England, and his absence looks set to continue for some time following a painful toe fracture sustained during the match. SUBSCRIBE NOW!The southpaw picked up the injury when an attempt to reverse sweep off Chris Woakes struck his right toe, forcing him to play through discomfort before being sidelined.PollDo you think Rishabh Pant will make a successful comeback after his injury?Absolutely!Maybe, depends on recovery.Uncertain, injuries are tough.On Wednesday, Pant took to Instagram to vent his frustration, posting a picture of his plastered foot on his story with the blunt caption: “I hate this sooo much.” But while the frustration is real, Pant has found a lighter way to pass the time — in the kitchen. Earlier in the day, the 27-year-old shared a playful social media video of himself baking pizzas, limping slightly yet grinning in a chef's apron as he kneaded dough and arranged toppings.“Ghar pe to kuch banaya nahi hai, yaha pizza bana raha hai” (“I haven't cooked anything at home, and here I am making pizza”), Pant quipped, adding an Italian flourish to his caption: “Impasto, salsa, forno… and me.”In the clip, Pant told viewers, “Today I'm going to show you how to make pizza… I think I'm gonna make vegetarian pizza. With the truffle? Yeah, with the truffle.”Ever the entertainer, he joked mid-session: “Can I use your scissor, please? Where is it? Pizza is getting ready. I'm going to wait for it. Very hot in here, boys and girls.” He signed off with his trademark chuckle: “That's the only thing I can do with a broken foot right now. Mother must be thinking — ghar pe to kuch banaya nahi hai, yaha pizza bana raha hai.”Watch:While his comeback date remains uncertain, Pant's latest posts prove that even in recovery, he's keeping his spirits high.Catch Rani Rampal's inspiring story on Game On, Episode 4.
