BTS members at LA's morning beach BTS, the global K-pop sensation, is set to return as a full group in 2026 following the completion of mandatory military service by all members. The group announced their hiatus in 2022 to fulfill national duties, with the eldest member Jin discharged in June 2024, followed by others progressively. According to statements from their agency HYBE, the comeback will mark a significant milestone, allowing the septet to resume activities together. This development comes as fans have eagerly awaited updates, with solo projects keeping the momentum during the break.Working on new album in LA Currently, the members are engaged in daily practice sessions in Los Angeles, honing their skills for the upcoming release. These sessions focus on choreography, vocal training, and new song arrangements, ensuring a polished performance. Sources close to the group indicate that this intensive preparation is taking place in a professional studio environment, blending their signature style with fresh concepts. The LA location provides a creative hub away from distractions, allowing for focused collaboration. Details on music and potential activities The 2026 comeback is expected to feature a new album with tracks that evolve from their previous works. Industry insiders suggest possible inclusions of innovative elements like advanced production techniques. While specifics remain under wraps, the group has hinted at themes of growth and unity in recent interviews. Additionally, there could be plans for concerts and fan engagements, building on their history of successful global tours, though details are yet to be confirmed.Fan expectations and group impact Fan communities, known as ARMY, are expressing high anticipation through social media discussions and fan events. This comeback is poised to reinforce BTS's influence in the music industry, where K-pop has seen consistent growth in global streams, as reported by Billboard.
