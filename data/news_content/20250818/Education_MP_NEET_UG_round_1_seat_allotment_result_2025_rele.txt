MP NEET UG counselling 2025: The Directorate of Medical Education (DME), Madhya Pradesh, on Monday released the NEET UG 2025 Round 1 seat allotment results for admission into Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS), and Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS) courses. Candidates can now check their allotment status on the official website, dme.mponline.gov.in.As per the revised counselling schedule, students allotted seats are required to report to their designated colleges between August 19 and August 23, 2025. The window for seat cancellation will remain open until August 24, while the option for seat upgradation is available until August 23.Candidates applying for upgradation must also submit an affidavit of ₹500 affirming rural service obligations and complete the seat leaving bond if their seat is not upgraded.For the 2025 academic session, a total of 2,638 MBBS seats have been allocated in government medical colleges, while 2,200 MBBS seats are available in private institutions. Additionally, 1,170 BDS seats are being offered across private dental colleges in the state.MP NEET UG round 1 seat allotment 2025: Criteria for seat allotmentThe seat allocation has been carried out based on the following parameters:Candidate’s NEET merit rankPreferences filled during counsellingApplicable reservation guidelinesAvailability of seatsMP NEET UG 2025 seat allotment result: Steps to downloadCandidates can follow these steps to access their seat allotment status online:Visit the official website of MP DME: dme.mponline.gov.inOn the homepage, click on the link that reads “NEET UG 2025 Seat Allotment Result”Enter your NEET roll number, application ID, and date of birth in the login windowYour allotment result will appear on the screenDownload the result and take a printout for submission during college reportingAlternatively, candidates can click on the link provided here to download the MP NEET UG seat allotment result 2025.Candidates are advised to stay tuned to the official website to get the complete details of the MP NEET UG counselling result.Ready to navigate global policies?
