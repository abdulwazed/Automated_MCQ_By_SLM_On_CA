Gandalf-Frodo to return in 'Hunt for Gollum'
Ian McKellen hinted at Gandalf and Frodo's potential return in 'The Lord of the Rings: The Hunt for Gollum' during a fan event in London. McKellen teased the film's production, directed by Andy Serkis, and revealed the presence of characters named Frodo and Gandalf. While Elijah Wood attended the event, McKellen remained tight-lipped about Wood reprising his role.

It's time to travel back to Middle-earth once again, and this time around, we'll have some familiar faces joining this epic journey. Hollywood legend, Ian McKellen on Sunday, teased fans about the return of Gandalf and Frodo in the upcoming 'The Lord of the Rings: The Hunt for Gollum'.Gandalf and Frodo's ReturnThe revelation came during McKellen’s appearance at the For the Love of Fantasy fan event in London. In videos doing the rounds from the event, the actor took over the microphone to tease fans saying, “I hear there’s going to be another movie based in Middle-earth." Revealing production details, he added, "it's going to start filming in May. It’s going to be directed by Gollum (Andy Serkis), and it’s all about Gollum.”McKellen then went on to drop the biggest surprise of the event by saying, “I’ll tell you two secrets about the casting: There’s a character in the movie called Frodo, and there’s a character in the movie called Gandalf.” When pressed for more details, the actor quipped, “Apart from that, my lips are sealed.”LOTR Cast ReunitesThough the actor stopped short of confirming whether fellow actor Elijah Wood would reprise his role, Wood was in attendance at the event. The 'LOTR' cast also had a mini-reunion at the event, with franchise stars Sean Astin, Dominic Monaghan, Billy Boyd, and John Rhys-Davies also joining the event. About The FilmOriginally announced in 2023, the project is being directed by and starring Serkis, was initially slated for a 2026 release. However, the film is now expected to arrive in December 2027.Since concluding the original Oscar-winning trilogy in 2003 with 'The Return of the King', McKellen, Wood, and Peter Jackson reunited for 'The Hobbit' films between 2012 and 2014. If they reprise their roles in 'The Hunt for Gollum', it would mark yet another outing for the stars in the J.R.R.
