In the ever-evolving landscape of Indian cinema, where the box office is increasingly dominated by pan-India spectacles, one actor has carved a space unlike any other: Prabhas. Since the historic success of Baahubali 2: The Conclusion in 2017, Prabhas has become the face of opening day euphoria. No matter the genre, no matter the competition, his films have consistently stormed into theatres with earth-shattering day-one numbers, something even other superstars, despite their stature and talent, have struggled to replicate.And post Baahubali 2, the actor has not done even one sequel thereby taking out the last film‚Äôs buzz into account.Baahubali 2 didn‚Äôt just change Prabhas‚Äôs career; it changed the very grammar of Indian box office reporting. The SS Rajamouli-directed epic opened to record numbers, rewriting the rules of how big a film could go. It was the first film to cross the Rs 1000 crore mark. It also cemented Prabhas as the first genuine pan-India superstar from the South, with fan bases cutting across linguistic and geographical boundaries. Suddenly, Day 1 numbers became the currency of stardom, and every new big-ticket release was compared to the Baahubali phenomenon.The Day 1 StreakWhat makes Prabhas unique is his ability to sustain the ‚Äúevent film‚Äù aura beyond Baahubali. The film‚Äôs scale, action set pieces, and Prabhas‚Äôs aura carried it to one of the biggest first days for an Indian film at the time.The film earned over Rs 300 crore at the box office. Radhe Shyam: A rare miss, this romantic drama marked a departure from Prabhas‚Äôs mass-action image. With a softer genre and limited appeal in the Hindi belt, the film managed Rs 43.1 crore on opening day‚Äîa number still respectable, but far from the benchmark audiences expected.The film folded up at Rs 104 crore.Adipurush: In spite of polarizing reactions and social media criticism, Om Raut‚Äôs mythological epic earned a massive Rs 86.75 crore on its opening day.
