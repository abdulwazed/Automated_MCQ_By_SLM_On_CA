The Bihar Public Service Commission (BPSC) has officially released the schedule for the Assistant Section Officer (ASO) Preliminary Examination 2025, sparking keen interest among aspirants across the state. As per the official schedule released, the preliminary exam will be conducted on September 10, 2025 in a single shift. With only 41 vacancies available, competition is expected to be intense. The exam will be held offline, testing candidates on a range of subjects relevant to administrative functions. Graduates from recognised universities who meet the age criteria can apply.BPSC ASO vacancies and selection processThe BPSC ASO 2025 recruitment offers a total of 41 coveted positions, making it a highly competitive examination. The selection process is designed to assess candidates comprehensively and includes three key stages:Preliminary Examination: This is the first stage, serving as a screening test to shortlist candidates for the mains.Main Examination: Candidates who qualify in the preliminary round appear for the mains, which evaluates in-depth knowledge and analytical skills.Document Verification: Successful candidates from the mains undergo document verification to confirm eligibility and educational qualifications before final appointment.This multi-stage process ensures that only the most capable and well-prepared candidates are selected for the role of Assistant Section Officer.BPSC ASO admit card 2025The BPSC ASO 2025 admit cards are expected to be released in early September 2025. Candidates must download their admit cards from the official BPSC website: bpsc.bihar.gov.in well before the exam date.Important Tips for Aspirants:Download Early: Avoid last-minute technical issues by accessing the admit card as soon as it is released.Verify Details: Carefully check all personal information, exam center details, and instructions.Print Multiple Copies: Keep at least two printed copies for reference on the exam day.Possessing a valid admit card is mandatory for entry to the exam hall, making it one of the most crucial documents for aspirants.Ready to navigate global policies?
