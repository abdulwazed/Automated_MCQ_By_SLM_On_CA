The actor is currently busy with the promotional interviews for the same. During one such conversation, the actor expressed that he is disheartened that his movie is not releasing in theatres. On the other hand, he also shared his thoughts about the massive success of the film 'Saiyaara'.John Abraham gives credit to THIS person for the success of 'Saiyaara'In an interview with India Today, John expressed that he is happy to know that the film is doing "so well." The 'Pathaan' actor stated that he would give credit to the director of the film, Mohit Suri. According to John, Mohit went all out for the film.EXCLUSIVE: Mohit Suri Spills On 20 Years In Bollywood - Ahaan & Aneet, Alia Bhatt Dream Collab & MoreAdmiring the filmmaker, the actor further shared that he is in "love with Mohit as a person." Calling the director a "darling," John added that everyone said that they are celebrating his success.PollDo you think 'Saiyaara' deserves the success it has achieved?Absolutely, great film!Not really, overrated.John Abraham went on to say, "So, hats off to him. But major credit to Aditya Chopra for getting a film out theatrically with two newcomers." Praising the producer, the actor further said, "His faith in newcomers worked."John didn't stop there, as he wished more success for the film. Expressing his love for cinema halls, he said, "I want all these films to run because I want people to come back to the theatres."More about 'Tehran'Co-produced by Dinesh Vijan, 'Tehran' also stars Neeru Bajwa and Manushi Chhillar in key roles. As per the reports, the movie is based on the 2012 attacks on Israeli diplomats. Directed by Arun Gopalan, the film is scheduled to release on the OTT platform on August 14, 2025.More about 'Saiyaara'The Mohit Suri-directed and Aditya Chopra-backed film stars Ahaan Panday and Aneet Padda in the lead roles. The film has raked in Rs 318 crore in India until now.
