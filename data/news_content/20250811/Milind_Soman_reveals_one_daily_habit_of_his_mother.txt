Whether it is his fit frame, or his awe inspiring fitness videos, the actor goes on to prove that growing older has nothing to do with feeling older. Recently he took to his IG handle to share a family video of himself, his wife Ankita and mom Usha Soman who are all skipping together. Along with the video he revealed that at 86, his Aai (mom) skips everyday for health and fitness. He said, “Family skipping time. Aai (Mother) is now 86, and skipping is something she does every day, apart from yoga and other movements. Long life, health and happiness to all,” Milind wrote in the caption of the post.5 benefits of skipping ropeSkipping rope, though basic, delivers various health advantages to people. Regular rope skipping practices both enhance fitness while delivering benefits to your heart together with your muscles and are also good for your mental health, when combined with other activities. Here's how...Boosts cardiovascular healthThe physical activity of jumping rope is an excellent cardiovascular exercise, which develops both heart and lung strength. Your heart beats faster during rope jumping, which leads to better blood circulation and stronger heart muscles. Research shows that consistent rope skipping practice lowers blood pressure while reducing heart disease risks, through improved lung and heart performance. Your body will function more efficiently, because skipping rope enhances your cardiovascular ability, which supports extended health and active life.Improves coordination and balanceTo jump rope successfully, you need to maintain rhythm while controlling your body movements at precise times. Your brain and muscles work together during skipping, to maintain your balance and coordination. The activity develops your total motor abilities and boosts your agility levels, as well as your reaction speed. Scientific research demonstrates that rope skipping enhances balance, coordination and athletic performance amongst children and athletes. Not only that, the improved coordination helps decrease the chances of falls and daily accidents that lead to injuries. (Research Journal of Pharmacy and Technology – Effect of skipping rope exercise on physical fitness, Elite Jumps Blog – Surprising Benefits of Jumping Rope)Strengthens muscles and bonesThe physical activity of skipping rope works all major muscle groups starting from your calves and thighs, then moves to your core and finally engages your arms. Your muscles will become stronger while developing better definition, when you jump. The repetitive jumping motion during skipping rope exercises, delivers mild bone-stimulating pressure, which leads to bone growth while reducing osteoporosis risk. The regular practice of rope skipping, enhances your leg muscle endurance and maintains bone health, which together create physical strength.Burns calories and aids weight lossThe high-intensity nature of rope skipping makes it an effective workout which burns calories swiftly. You can easily burn 10-15 calories per minute, when you skip rope based on how fast and intensely you jump. This activity works well for weight loss and maintaining proper body weight distribution. Rope skipping engages your entire body, which stimulates metabolic processes while providing daily fitness benefits through brief exercise sessions. (PMC – The effect of an 8-week rope skipping intervention on performance, Cleveland Clinic – 6 Benefits of Jumping Rope)Enhances mental health and reduces stressThe practice of skipping rope delivers dual benefits to both physical health, and mental wellness. Physical exercise boosts endorphin production which creates positive mood effects, while decreasing stress levels. The coordinated movements required for skipping create mental clarity and improved concentration ability.
