He will headline a celebrity doubles match on October 10. This gives fans another chance to see him play. Federer expressed his happiness to return to Shanghai. He fondly remembers the city's great fans and unforgettable moments. Tennis legend Roger Federer and his wife Mirka sit in the Royal Box on Centre Court on day eight of the Wimbledon Tennis Championships in London, Monday, July 7, 2025. (AP Photo/Kin Cheung) Roger Federer will make a return to Shanghai this fall, headlining the “Roger Federer & Friends” celebrity doubles match on October 10, giving fans another opportunity to watch him in action."Hello, this is Roger, and I am really happy to be returning to Qizhong Stadium in Shanghai for the Rolex Shanghai Masters,” Federer said in a video.Go Beyond The Boundary with our YouTube channel. SUBSCRIBE NOW!“Shanghai has always been a special place for me, with great fans, unforgettable memories, and a real love for the game,” he added.The two-time Shanghai Masters champion last visited the city in 2024, spending a few fun-filled days exploring, meeting Novak Djokovic, watching Jannik Sinner, and even training indoors with Carlos Alcaraz, jokingly questioning what he was doing there.Federer boasts a 23-6 career record in Shanghai across eight appearances. On his debut in 2010, he defeated Novak Djokovic before falling to Andy Murray in the final. The same duo met in the 2012 semi-final, with Murray again prevailing in straight sets.PollWhich of Federer's past performances in Shanghai stands out to you the most?His 2014 title winHis 2017 championshipHis debut in 2010After a loss to Gael Monfils in 2013, Federer finally lifted his first title in China in 2014, defeating Djokovic and Gilles Simon. He was upset by Alberto Ramos-Vinolas in 2015 but returned in 2017 to claim his second Shanghai crown, beating Juan Martin del Potro and Rafael Nadal, and breaking Nadal’s serve three times in a dominant 6-4, 6-3 final.Federer’s later campaigns saw mixed results — a semi-final defeat to Borna Coric in 2018 and a quarter-final loss to Alexander Zverev in 2019, marking his last competitive appearance at the event.Last year, the Swiss maestro was warmly received in Shanghai, and in just two months, fans will once again see him grace the courts in one of his favorite tennis destinations.Catch Rani Rampal's inspiring story on Game On, Episode 4.
