Actor, producer, and Tamil Nadu Deputy Chief Minister Udhayanidhi Stalin congratulated Superstar Rajinikanth on completing 50 remarkable years in the film industry. In a heartfelt post on X, he shared that he had the privilege of watching Rajinikanth’s much-anticipated film 'Coolie' ahead of its release and thoroughly enjoyed the power-packed mass entertainer. Udhayanidhi described the film as a treat for fans and expressed his confidence that it would win hearts across the globe.Udhayanidhi congratulates Rajinikanth, praises 'Coolie'In his congratulatory note, he extended his best wishes not only to Rajinikanth but also to the entire team behind the film. I am truly delighted to congratulate our superstar, @rajinikanth sir, on completing 50 glorious years in the film industry. Had the opportunity to get an early glimpse of his much-awaited movie #Coolie, releasing tomorrow. I thoroughly enjoyed this power-packed mass entertainer and am sure it will capture the hearts of audiences everywhere. "My heartfelt wishes for a roaring success to @rajinikanth sir, @sunpictures, #Sathyaraj sir, @Dir_Lokesh, #AamirKhan sir, @iamnagarjuna sir, @nimmaupendra sir, @anirudhofficial, #SoubinShahir, @shrutihaasan, and the entire team behind this movie," wrote Udhayanidhi Stalin to tease fans about 'Coolie.'Global hype as 'Coolie' readies for release'Coolie' is creating tremendous enthusiasm among the audience as it prepares for global release in more than 100 countries. The film stars a star-stamped actor, in which Rajinikanth played an important role with Indian cinema stalwarts like Nagarjuna, Sathyaraj, Aamir Khan, Upendra, Soubin Shahir, and Shruti Haasan. The recently released trailer has further enhanced the fan expectations, showing Rajinikanth's character helping his friend Satyaraj's character in a stressful position, despite his on-screen daughter's opposition, Shruti Haasan.NAGARJUNA UNLEASHED: Villain Role in ‘Coolie’ Was LIBERATING Like Never BeforeCensor cleared, fans celebrate Rajinikanth’s Golden JubileeWith a 2-hour and 50-minute runtime, 'Coolie' received a certificate from the censor board.
