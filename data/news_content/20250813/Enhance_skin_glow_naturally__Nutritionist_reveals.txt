Nutritionist Anjali Mukerjee highlights that glowing skin isn’t just about creams or treatments—it starts with your diet. She recommends a simple, homemade juice packed with antioxidants, vitamins, and essential nutrients that help combat fine lines, dullness, and sagging. By nourishing your body from the inside, this juice supports skin regeneration, improves elasticity, and fights oxidative stress caused by free radicals. Regular consumption can enhance hydration, brighten complexion, and slow visible signs of ageing. Integrating such nutrient-rich beverages into your daily routine offers a natural, affordable, and effective approach to long-term skin health.How diet and antioxidants can slow visible signs of ageing with these powerful superfoodsAgeing is a natural process, but the speed at which your body shows signs of ageing—such as wrinkles, dark spots, and loss of skin elasticity—can be influenced by diet. Foods rich in antioxidants help combat oxidative stress and protect skin cells, potentially delaying visible signs of ageing.Nutritionist Anjali Mukerjee highlights that incorporating antioxidant-rich ingredients like amla (Indian gooseberry), pomegranate, and black grapes into your diet can strengthen the skin’s natural defense system and enhance collagen production, essential for firm, youthful skin.https://www.instagram.com/p/DNKcRRTxtEL/ Why amla, beetroot, and grapes are anti-aging powerhousesBeetroot: Boost circulation and detoxifyBeetroot is packed with vitamin C, iron, and nitrates, which improve blood circulation, enhance skin elasticity, and aid the body’s detoxification process. Improved blood flow ensures that nutrients reach skin cells efficiently, giving the skin an inner glow and youthful vitality.Amla: Collagen booster and free radical fighterAmla is a staple in traditional Indian health practices, revered for its high vitamin C content and potent antioxidant properties. It helps in collagen synthesis, reduces oxidative stress, and supports cellular repair. Together, these benefits slow down the appearance of fine lines, sagging, and pigmentation.Black grapes and pomegranate: Skin-protective nutrientsBlack grapes and pomegranate are rich in polyphenols and antioxidants, which work synergistically with amla and beetroot to neutralize free radicals, prevent cellular damage, and promote skin hydration and elasticity.Homemade anti-ageing juice recipeIngredients:1 small amla (Indian gooseberry)1 cup pomegranate seeds1 cup black grapesBlack salt and chat masala to tastePreparation steps:Grate the deseeded amla and strain it to extract juice.Juice the black grapes and pomegranate separately.Mix all the juices together.Add a pinch of black salt and chat masala for taste.Serve immediately for maximum freshness and potency.Pro tip: Consume this juice within 20 minutes of preparation to retain its antioxidant properties.How this juice fights skin ageingFree radicals are unstable molecules that can damage skin cells, accelerating ageing symptoms like fine lines, pigmentation, and sagging. They are produced naturally through metabolism, environmental exposure, and UV radiation.Antioxidants found in amla, beetroot, pomegranate, and black grapes neutralize free radicals, protect cellular structures, and nourish the skin from the inside out. Regular consumption, combined with a healthy diet, exercise, and proper sleep, can significantly slow down visible ageing while improving overall well-being.Additional tips for healthy, youthful skinStay hydrated: Drink plenty of water to maintain skin elasticity.Balanced diet: Include whole grains, nuts, seeds, and leafy greens for nutrients that support skin health.Regular exercise: Improves circulation, which boosts nutrient delivery to skin cells.Adequate sleep: Allows cellular repair and rejuvenation, essential for youthful skin.About the AuthorTOI Lifestyle DeskThe TOI Lifestyle Desk is a dynamic team of dedicated journalists who, with unwavering passion and commitment, sift through the pulse of the nation to curate a vibrant tapestry of lifestyle news for The Times of India readers.
