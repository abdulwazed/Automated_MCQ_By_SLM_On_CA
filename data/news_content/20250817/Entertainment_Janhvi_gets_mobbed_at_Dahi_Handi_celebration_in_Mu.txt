Janhvi Kapoor is currently on a promotional spree for her upcoming film 'Param Sundari.' And for the same, she attended a Dahi Handi celebration in Mumbai on Saturday (August 16). She even addressed the crowd in Marathi and urged them to watch her movie coming to theaters. However, things changed when she was exiting the venue, as she got mobbed by the crowd. The video of the same has gone viral now.Here's what happened.Janhvi Kapoor gets mobbed during Janmashtami celebrationFor the occasion, the actress wore a traditional outfit in pastel yellow. Exuding the festive vibes, the actress looked absolutely beautiful in her attire. The actress broke the handi with a coconut at the event. Not just that, she also greeted and wished the crowd gathered in Marathi, wishing them on Janmashtami.However, things went out of control when people came to catch a glimpse of the actor. It almost turned into a mob-like situation. However, the actress didn't lose her calm as she walked towards her car. In the video posted by paparazzo Viral Bhayan's account, she looked visibly uncomfortable. Her guards came to rescue and escorted Janhvi to the car. She waved at the fans and quickly jumped into her vehicle.Check out the video here.More about 'Param Sundari''Param Sundari' stars Janhvi Kapoor alongside Sidharth Malhotra. The romantic drama is reportedly set in Delhi and Kerala. While almost everyone loved the chemistry of the lead pair, the trailer, and the song, the film got involved in a controversy due to a church scene.A Christian group has demanded removal of the flirty church scene from the film and other promotional videos. On the other hand, some even questioned Janhvi's Malayali accent in the film.Apart from the duo, it also stars Rajeev Khandelwal and Aakash Dahiya in key roles.Directed by Tushar Jalota, the film is set to hit theaters on August 29, 2025."Get the latest news updates on Times of India, including reviews of the movie Coolie and War 2."About the AuthorTOI Entertainment DeskThe TOI Entertainment Desk is a dynamic and dedicated team of journalists, working tirelessly to bring the pulse of the entertainment world straight to the readers of The Times of India.
