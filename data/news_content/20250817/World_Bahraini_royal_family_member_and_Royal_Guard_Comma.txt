Bahraini royal family member and Royal Guard Commander claims victory at UK’s Rushford Endurance Race
Sheikh Nasser bin Hamad Al Khalifa triumphed in the 120km Rushford Endurance Race in the UK, leading Bahrain's Royal Endurance Team to victory. Despite a last-minute venue change, the team showcased remarkable adaptability and teamwork. Sheikh Nasser's win underscores Bahrain's prominence in international endurance sport and his leadership in equestrian excellence.

Sheikh Nasser has claimed victory in the challenging 120km Rushford Endurance Race/Image: Instagram His Highness Sheikh Nasser bin Hamad Al Khalifa, Representative of His Majesty the King for Humanitarian Work and Youth Affairs and Commander of Bahrain’s Royal Guard, has claimed victory in the challenging 120km Rushford Endurance Race held in the United Kingdom. The win marks yet another milestone for the Bahraini Royal Endurance Team and reinforces Bahrain’s status as a powerhouse in international endurance sport. The Rushford International Endurance Race is a prestigious, multi-stage equestrian event held in the United Kingdom, attracting elite endurance riders from across the globe. The race features challenging categories, including the 100km, 120km, and 160km distances designed to test the stamina, strategy, and partnership between horse and rider.TL;DR:Sheikh Nasser won the 120km Rushford Endurance Race in the UK, showcasing leadership and equestrian excellence.The Bahrain Royal Endurance Team managed a swift entry and logistics after last-minute changes, displaying professionalism and agility.The event brought together elite riders from Bahrain, UAE, UK, Spain, and India, with Sheikh Nasser powering through all four stages of the race.Race day: Victory and performanceCompeting on his horse Schelmigra Truckstarr, Sheikh Nasser successfully completed the four demanding stages of the 120km event, demonstrating not only physical stamina but strategic racing acumen. The Rushford International Endurance Race required rigorous veterinary checks, careful preparation and precise riding; a challenge that Sheikh Nasser and his team met head-on. His achievement is especially impressive given Bahrain’s late venue change (originally scheduled for France), and the logistical effort required to reroute the team and horses to the UK within just 72 hours.Sheikh Nasser’s victory was hailed by endurance racing officials, fellow competitors, and Bahrain’s sporting community, reinforcing his position as a world-class endurance athlete and leader.The Royal Endurance Team’s journeyUnder Sheikh Nasser’s leadership, Bahrain’s Royal Endurance Team continues to stand out on the international stage. The team displayed remarkable adaptability and teamwork as they secured entry into the Rushford competition at short notice, completed all required medical and veterinary checks, and held focused training sessions ahead of the race.Bahrain’s riders, including those who tackled the 160km and 100km distances, gained valuable experience and reaffirmed the team’s commitment to podium finishes and ongoing sporting development.About HH Sheikh Nasser bin Hamad Al KhalifaBorn in 1987, Sheikh Nasser bin Hamad Al Khalifa is a member of Bahrain’s royal family, Commander of the Royal Guard, Chairman of Bapco Energies, and influential sports leader. He is known for his advocacy of youth, humanitarian initiatives, and his leadership in promoting endurance sports nationally and internationally.
