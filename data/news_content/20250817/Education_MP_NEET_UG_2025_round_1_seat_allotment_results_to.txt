The Directorate of Medical Education (DME), Madhya Pradesh, will announce the Madhya Pradesh National Eligibility cum Entrance Test Undergraduate (MP NEET UG) 2025 Round 1 seat allotment results tomorrow, August 18, 2025. Candidates can access the allotment results on the official website: dme.mponline.gov.in. Once the MP NEET UG 2025 Round 1 allotment results are declared, candidates must report to their allotted colleges from August 19 to 23, 2025, according to the revised schedule. Students can also opt for seat cancellation until August 24 and seat upgradation until August 23. How to download MP NEET UG 2025 seat allotment result Candidates can follow these steps to download their MP NEET UG 2025 seat allotment result in PDF format:  Visit the official DME Madhya Pradesh website: dme.mponline.gov.in.   Look for the link titled “MP NEET UG Counselling 2025 Seat Allotment Result”.  Submit the required information, if any, to view your allotment result.   Download and print the PDF for future reference and college reporting.MP NEET UG 2025: Seat allotment criteria Seats are allotted based on:  Merit rank   Preferences filled by the candidate   Reservation criteria   Seat availability   Other relevant factors   The allotment list includes details such as the candidate's information, allotted institute, and allotted course. MP NEET UG 2025: Seat availability For MBBS admissions, a total of 2,638 seats are available in government medical colleges and 2,200 seats in private medical colleges. Additionally, 1,170 BDS seats are offered in private dental colleges.MP NEET UG 2025: Important instructions for candidates DME Madhya Pradesh has instructed that students opting for seat upgradation must submit an affidavit of Rs 500 regarding rural service and complete the seat leaving bond if the seat is not upgraded.Note: Candidates are also advised to keep their documents ready for verification and report to their respective colleges within the prescribed timeline.MP NEET UG 2025: Updates on withheld results The results of 79 UG candidates were previously withheld due to a petition concerning exam centre issues in Indore and Ujjain. They were allowed to participate in the counselling process after the National Testing Agency (NTA) declared their results.
