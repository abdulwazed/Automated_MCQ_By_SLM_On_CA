'The value of a human life should be more than sports'- Ex India cricketer questions India-Pakistan clash

Manoj Tiwary questions the India-Pakistan Asia Cup match appropriateness after the Pahalgam attack and Operation Sindoor, emphasizing human life's value over sports. He also expressed surprise over Shreyas Iyer and Yashasvi Jaiswal's exclusion from the Asia Cup squad, despite their strong performances.

India vs Pakistan T20 game (X- Hardik Pandya) Former Indian cricketer Manoj Tiwary has expressed concerns about the upcoming India-Pakistan cricket match at the Asia Cup 2025, questioning its appropriateness in light of the recent Pahalgam terror attack and subsequent Operation Sindoor. The match scheduling has sparked debate, particularly after Indian legends recently boycotted their games against Pakistan in the World Championships of Legends due to bilateral tensions.India is set to begin their Asia Cup campaign against the UAE on September 10, followed by the highly anticipated match against Pakistan on September 14 in Dubai. Their final group stage match will be against Oman on September 19 in Abu Dhabi.EXCLUSIVE: India to boycott Pakistan in Asia Cup? After the Pahalgam attack, in which so many innocent civilians were killed, and then the war that followed, a lot of talk was going on that this time we will give a befitting reply. Despite this, after a few months, everything has been forgotten... I find it hard to believe that this match is happening, that the value of a human life can be zero. What do they want to achieve by playing with Pakistan?... The value of a human life should be more than sports... There is no question of me watching the match at all..."
